Generative AI: A double-edged sword for creative thinking learning

生成式AI：創意學習的雙面刃

——來自面部表情與fNIRS的證據

Evidence from facial expressions and fNIRS

Highlights 研究亮點

  • Facial and neural data classify creativity with 91.4% accuracy using CatBoost.
    面部與神經數據以91.4%準確率使用CatBoost分類創意表現。
  • GAI boosts creative performance but increases fear emotions.
    生成式AI提升創意表現，但增加恐懼情緒。
  • GAI-aided brainstorming activates right DLPFC; TRIZ activates bilateral DLPFC.
    AI輔助頭腦風暴活化右側DLPFC；TRIZ活化雙側DLPFC。
  • GAI enhances novelty in brainstorming and practicality in TRIZ design.
    AI提升頭腦風暴的新穎性與TRIZ設計的實用性。
  • GAI reduces the depth of design thinking in brainstorming.
    AI降低頭腦風暴中設計思維的深度。

Grammar 文法重點

1. 現在分詞（-ing）作形容詞：
• generative AI-assisted creative thinking education（AI輔助的創意教育）
• fear emitting emotions（恐懼情緒）
2. 被動語態：
• both facial expressions were collected（面部表情被收集）
• data was analyzed using CatBoost（數據被分析）
3. 複合形容詞：
• generative AI-based learning tools（基於生成式AI的學習工具）

Abstract 內容（一）

  • With the widespread integration of generative AI tools into educational contexts, understanding their influence on learners' cognitive and emotional processes has become increasingly critical.
    隨著生成式AI工具廣泛整合入教育情境，了解其對學習者認知與情緒歷程的影響變得越來越重要。
  • While AI holds potential for enhancing creativity, its double-edged impact on neurocognitive and emotional processes still requires further investigation.
    雖然AI有潛力提升創意，但其對神經認知與情緒歷程的雙面影響仍需進一步研究。
  • This study investigates the impact of generative AI-based learning tools on the creative thinking learning process.
    本研究探討生成式AI學習工具對創意思維學習歷程的影響。
  • Participants were divided into two groups: a generative AI design group and a traditional design group.
    參與者被分為兩組：生成式AI設計組與傳統設計組。
  • They completed tasks employing the divergent brainstorming creative method and the structured innovation TRIZ method.
    他們完成採用發散性頭腦風暴創意法與結構化創新TRIZ方法的任務。

Abstract 內容（二）

  • During these tasks, both facial expressions and functional near-infrared spectroscopy (fNIRS) data were collected to explore the effects of generative AI-assisted creative thinking education on students' facial emotional changes and prefrontal cortex (PFC) activation patterns.
    在這些任務期間，收集面部表情與功能性近紅外光譜數據，以探討AI輔助創意思維教育對學生面部情緒變化與前額葉皮層活化模式的影響。
  • Expert evaluations were conducted to assess the outcomes of creative thinking.
    進行專家評估以評估創意思維的成果。
  • The results indicated that generative AI significantly enhanced creative thinking performance.
    結果顯示生成式AI顯著提升創意思維表現。
  • Facial emotion analysis revealed that, with generative AI assistance, the brainstorming process generated more fear emotions, while the Theory of Inventive Problem Solving (TRIZ) design process produced more happiness emotions.
    面部情緒分析顯示，在AI輔助下，頭腦風暴產生更多恐懼情緒，而TRIZ設計產生更多快樂情緒。

Abstract 內容（三）

  • fNIRS data showed that, with generative AI support, the brainstorming process facilitated activation in the right DLPFC, while the TRIZ design process activated both the left and right DLPFC areas.
    fNIRS數據顯示，在AI支援下，頭腦風暴促進右側DLPFC活化，而TRIZ設計活化左右兩側DLPFC。
  • Machine learning classifiers indicated that facial emotion and fNIRS data could serve as effective indicators for assessing creative thinking performance.
    機器學習分類器指出面部情緒與fNIRS數據可作為評估創意思維表現的有效指標。
  • The CatBoost classifier achieved an accuracy rate of 91.40%/89.06% in the two groups.
    CatBoost分類器在兩組中達到91.40%/89.06%的準確率。
  • This study focuses on learners' facial emotions and PFC activity, revealing that while generative AI enhances creative thinking performance, it may also increase negative emotions.
    本研究聚焦於學習者的情緒與PFC活動，揭示生成式AI雖提升創意思維表現，但也可能增加負面情緒。

Summary 總結

主要發現：

  • AI 提升創意表現，但增加恐懼情緒
  • AI 輔助頭腦風暴 → 右側DLPFC活化
  • AI 輔助TRIZ → 雙側DLPFC活化
  • 面部表情 + 腦部數據 = 91.4% 準確率預測創意
